# Knowledge Management Program

Objectives

- Capture tacit knowledge, enable knowledge reuse, and reduce single-person dependencies.

Scope

- KM processes for engineering, operations, maintenance, and incident response.

Stakeholders

- Knowledge Manager, Content Owners, Trainers.

Deliverables

- KM playbook, expert directories, lessons-learned repository.

Processes

- After-action reviews, knowledge-capture interviews, and structured 'how-to' creation.

Governance Mechanisms

- Knowledge owners, periodic validation of critical knowledge assets.

Templates

- Interview guide, lessons-learned template, knowledge handover checklist.

Metrics

- Number of captured knowledge items, reuse rate, reduction in time-to-resolution.

Risks

- Knowledge decay, lack of incentives to contribute.

Mitigation Strategies

- Recognition program, small incentives, integrate capture into workflows.

Best Practices

- Capture knowledge close to the event, structure it for findability.
